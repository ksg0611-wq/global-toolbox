If you look at your YouTube Studio Analytics panel, you will find two metrics that heavily dictate your monthly payout: **CPM** and **RPM**.

For many new and intermediate creators, these terms look identical. However, understanding their differences is crucial for optimizing your video production schedule and maximizing your revenue streams.

---

## The Core Difference: Who Pays vs. Who Earns

To understand the core difference, look at who the metric belongs to:

- **CPM (Cost Per Mille)** represents the cost to the **advertiser**. It is the amount of money advertisers spend to serve 1,000 ad impressions on your channel.
- **RPM (Revenue Per Mille)** represents the earnings of the **creator**. It is the amount of money you actually take home per 1,000 total video views.

---

## Why RPM is Always Lower Than CPM

Many creators feel discouraged when they see an $8.00 CPM but only a $3.00 RPM. This gap is normal and is caused by three factors:

1. **The 45% Revenue Split**: YouTube takes a 45% platform fee from ad revenues. CPM shows the advertiser cost before this split; RPM shows your earnings after.
2. **Non-Monetized Views**: Not every view has ads. CPM is calculated based *only* on monetized views, while RPM is calculated based on *all* views (even those where viewers skipped or saw no ads).
3. **Multiple Revenue Inputs**: Unlike CPM, RPM is a holistic metric. It integrates alternative monetization tools such as Channel Memberships, Super Chats, Super Stickers, and YouTube Premium earnings, giving you a true snapshot of your channel's efficiency.

---

## 3 Ways to Raise Your Channel's RPM

If you want to earn more money without necessarily doubling your view count, focus on raising your RPM:

- **Target High-CPM Industries**: Create content covering topics advertisers pay top dollar for—such as SaaS reviews, online business models, personal finance, software development, or real estate.
- **Produce Longer Videos**: Videos longer than 8 minutes allow you to manually place **mid-roll ads**, creating multiple ad impressions per view.
- **Optimize for High-Tier Countries**: Tailor your video topics and titles to appeal to audiences in the United States, United Kingdom, Germany, or Australia. A small view count from these markets often pays much more than millions of views from lower-CPM countries.
